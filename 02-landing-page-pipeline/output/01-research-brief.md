# Stage 1: Vertical Research Brief — Fitness Studios & Personal Trainers

> Pipeline: Reservio Landing Page Pipeline

## 1. Market Overview

- **Global fitness market**: $96B+ (2024), projected $135B by 2028 (CAGR ~8%)
- **Studio/gym count**: ~200,000 fitness studios in Europe, ~40,000 in US alone
- **Typical business profile**:
  - Staff: 1-5 (personal trainers) or 3-15 (boutique studios)
  - Active clients: 100-500
  - Monthly revenue: €3,000-15,000
  - Monthly bookings: 200-2,000
- **Digital maturity**:
  - ~40% still use paper calendars, phone calls, or WhatsApp for bookings
  - ~35% use basic tools (Calendly, Google Calendar, Excel)
  - ~25% use dedicated fitness booking platforms (Mindbody, Fitogram)
- **Key trend**: Post-COVID hybrid model (in-studio + online classes) created urgent need for proper booking systems. Studios without online booking lost 20-30% of clients to competitors who had it.

## 2. Top 5 Booking Pain Points

### Pain #1: No-Shows and Late Cancellations (SEVERITY: CRITICAL)

- **Impact**: 15-25% revenue loss. Average studio loses €500-2,000/month in empty slots
- **Current workaround**: Manual text reminders (inconsistent), penalty policies (rarely enforced), overbooking (causes chaos when everyone shows up)
- **Emotional weight**: Frustration, feeling disrespected ("I held that slot for you"), resentment toward clients
- **Frequency**: Daily problem for most studios

### Pain #2: After-Hours Booking Demand (SEVERITY: HIGH)

- **Impact**: 60% of booking intent happens outside business hours (evenings, weekends). Studios without 24/7 booking lose this demand entirely
- **Current workaround**: Instagram DMs ("DM me to book"), WhatsApp messages, "Call tomorrow" voicemail
- **Emotional weight**: Anxiety about missed opportunities, feeling always "on"
- **Frequency**: Every evening/weekend

### Pain #3: Manual Scheduling Chaos (SEVERITY: HIGH)

- **Impact**: 5-10 hours/week spent on scheduling admin. Double-bookings, calendar conflicts, forgotten sessions
- **Current workaround**: Paper diary, WhatsApp threads, Google Calendar (shared but messy), Excel spreadsheets
- **Emotional weight**: Overwhelm, burnout, "I became a trainer to train, not to be a secretary"
- **Frequency**: Daily

### Pain #4: No Client Visibility (SEVERITY: MEDIUM)

- **Impact**: Can't personalize training, can't track attendance trends, can't identify clients about to churn, can't upsell effectively
- **Current workaround**: Memory, sticky notes, basic spreadsheets that nobody updates
- **Emotional weight**: Flying blind, feeling unprofessional compared to larger competitors
- **Frequency**: Ongoing — impact compounds over time

### Pain #5: Payment Friction (SEVERITY: MEDIUM)

- **Impact**: Delayed payments, cash-only limitations, package tracking headaches, awkward conversations
- **Current workaround**: Bank transfers, cash, Revolut/PayPal, manual counting of remaining sessions
- **Emotional weight**: Uncomfortable money conversations with clients they have personal relationships with
- **Frequency**: Weekly (billing cycles, package renewals)

## 3. Competitor Analysis

### Mindbody
- **Positioning**: Enterprise-grade fitness & wellness platform
- **Pricing**: $139-$699/month (plus setup fees)
- **Lead feature**: All-in-one: booking + POS + marketing + branded app
- **Strength**: Brand recognition, marketplace for consumers to discover studios
- **Messaging gap**: Overkill for small studios. Pricing is a barrier. Onboarding takes weeks, not minutes. Their target is chains, not independents.

### Acuity Scheduling (Squarespace)
- **Positioning**: General-purpose scheduling tool
- **Pricing**: $16-$49/month
- **Lead feature**: Calendar integration, clean UX
- **Strength**: Simple, affordable, well-integrated with Squarespace websites
- **Messaging gap**: Not fitness-specific. No group class booking. No client profiles. No attendance tracking. Feels generic.

### Fitogram
- **Positioning**: Boutique fitness studio management
- **Pricing**: €29-€99/month
- **Lead feature**: Class management with waitlists
- **Strength**: Purpose-built for fitness, strong in DACH region
- **Messaging gap**: Limited availability outside Germany/Austria/Switzerland. Weak payment processing. No free plan — high barrier to try.

### Bookee
- **Positioning**: Mobile-first fitness booking
- **Pricing**: Custom (opaque — requires sales call)
- **Lead feature**: Branded mobile app for studios
- **Strength**: Beautiful mobile experience, good for tech-forward studios
- **Messaging gap**: No transparent pricing. No free plan. Requires sales process — hostile to small operators who want to try before they buy.

### Calendly
- **Positioning**: Universal scheduling
- **Pricing**: $8-$16/month
- **Lead feature**: Simple link-based booking for 1-on-1 meetings
- **Strength**: Dead simple, everyone knows it
- **Messaging gap**: Zero fitness awareness. No group classes. No client database. No payments. No packages. A scheduling tool, not a business management tool.

### Reservio Opportunity

Position between "too simple" (Calendly, Acuity) and "too expensive/complex" (Mindbody):
- Full fitness features at SMB price point
- Free plan as risk-free entry
- Setup in minutes, not weeks
- Both group classes AND personal training in one tool

### Terminology Guide

**USE (insider language)**:
- Clients (not customers)
- Sessions (not appointments)
- Classes (not events)
- Studio (not facility)
- Trainer / Coach (not instructor — unless yoga/pilates)
- Book / Schedule (interchangeable)
- No-shows (universally understood pain term)

**AVOID (outsider language)**:
- Appointments (medical/corporate connotation)
- Customers (too transactional)
- Enterprise (signals "not for me")
- Platform / Solution (corporate jargon)
- Optimize / Leverage / Streamline (buzzword fatigue)

## 5. Decision Journey

### Trigger Event
- Lost a specific client who said "I went to [competitor] because they have online booking"
- Had 3+ no-shows in one week and felt the financial pain
- Spent a Sunday evening manually reorganizing Monday's schedule
- Hired a second trainer and realized paper scheduling doesn't scale
- Saw a competitor's Instagram with a "Book Now" button

### Research Behavior
1. Google search: "booking software for [their niche]"
2. Ask peers in Facebook groups / WhatsApp trainer groups: "What do you use?"
3. Check review sites: Capterra, G2, TrustPilot
4. Visit 2-3 competitor websites, compare features and pricing
5. Sign up for 1-2 free trials
6. Ask existing clients: "Would you use this?"

### Decision Maker
- **Solo trainers**: Owner is the only decision maker (100%)
- **Small studios**: Owner decides (90%), sometimes front desk manager has input
- **Boutique studios**: Owner decides with input from head trainer

### Timeline
- Trigger to active search: 1-7 days
- Active research: 3-7 days
- Free trial: 1-2 weeks
- Purchase decision: Within 30 days of trigger
- **Fast movers** (urgent pain): Sign up same day as search

### Top 3 Objections at Decision Point

1. **"Will my clients actually use it?"**
   - Root fear: Paying for something nobody adopts
   - Counter: 93% of consumers prefer businesses with online booking. Their clients already book restaurants, haircuts, and Ubers online.

2. **"I'm not technical enough to set this up"**
   - Root fear: Wasting time, looking stupid, giving up halfway
   - Counter: Setup in 15 minutes. If they can post on Instagram, they can set up Reservio. Support available.

3. **"I can't afford another monthly subscription"**
   - Root fear: Adding cost without clear ROI
   - Counter: Free plan exists. Even at €10/month, preventing 2 no-shows pays for itself. Frame as investment, not cost.
