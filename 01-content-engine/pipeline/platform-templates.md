# Platform Templates & Best Practices

## LinkedIn

### Post Template
```
[HOOK - 1-2 lines that create curiosity or state a contrarian view]

[CONTEXT - 2-3 sentences setting up the story/insight]

[BODY - 2-3 short paragraphs with the meat of the content]
- Use bullet points for lists
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences max

[TAKEAWAY - 1 sentence summarizing the key insight]

[CTA - Question to drive comments or discussion prompt]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2
```

### Best Practices
- **Hook matters most** -- only first 2 lines show before "See more"
- Post between 8-10 AM CET on weekdays (peak for European B2B)
- Tuesday-Thursday perform best for B2B content
- Respond to every comment in first 2 hours
- Tag people/companies mentioned (boosts distribution)
- Native documents/carousels get 3x more reach than link posts

### Content Types That Work
1. **Contrarian takes** ("Most SaaS holdings do X. Here's why we do Y.")
2. **Numbered lists** ("7 things we learned running 7 companies")
3. **Behind-the-scenes** ("Here's what our Monday all-hands looks like across 7 companies")
4. **Data-driven insights** ("We analyzed 20M bookings. Here's what surprised us.")
5. **Personal leadership posts** (CEO/CTO sharing honest reflections)

## Twitter/X

### Thread Template
```
1/N [BOLD OPENING STATEMENT - hook that stands alone as a tweet]

2/N [CONTEXT - why this matters]

3/N [POINT 1 - one clear idea with data or example]

4/N [POINT 2 - next idea, builds on previous]

5/N [POINT 3 - strongest point or surprising insight]

6/N [ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAY - what the reader can do]

7/N [CTA - follow, retweet, or link to longer content]
```

### Standalone Tweet Template
```
[Insight or observation in 1-2 sentences]

[Supporting data point or example]

[Optional: question or hot take]
```

### Best Practices
- **Thread tweet 1 must stand alone** -- most people only see this
- Post threads 12:00-14:00 CET (lunch engagement peak)
- Standalone tweets: 8-9 AM and 17-18 CET
- Use numbers in tweet 1 ("7 lessons", "20M bookings")
- Quote-tweet portfolio company announcements with Abugo perspective
- Pin best-performing thread weekly

### Content Types That Work
1. **Lesson threads** ("7 things we learned...")
2. **Contrarian takes** ("Hot take: headless commerce isn't about technology.")
3. **Data drops** ("We process 20M bookings/year at Reservio. The #1 no-show reason?")
4. **Quick tips** (One actionable insight from running commerce SaaS)
5. **Reaction to industry news** (fast takes on commerce/SaaS news)

## Newsletter

### Weekly Digest Template
```
Subject: [Compelling subject line -- question or number-driven]
Preview text: [First 50 chars that appear in inbox preview]

---

# Abugo Weekly: [Theme of the Week]

Hey [First Name],

[1-2 sentence personal opener connecting to the theme]

---

## The Big Idea
[200-300 words on the week's main theme/insight]

## From the Portfolio
[2-3 short updates from Abugo companies, 50-100 words each]
- **Reservio:** [update]
- **Smartsupp:** [update]
- **[Other]:** [update]

## Worth Reading
[3-5 curated links with 1-sentence commentary]
- [Article title](link) -- [why it matters]

## One Number
[Single striking stat from Abugo operations with brief context]

---

See you next week,
[Name], Abugo

[Unsubscribe] | [Share with a friend]
```

### Best Practices
- **Send Thursday 10:00 CET** (tested best for B2B in CEE)
- Subject line: question or number ("What 20M bookings taught us about no-shows")
- Keep total length under 800 words (3-min read)
- One clear CTA per section, not per paragraph
- Personal tone -- like a smart colleague forwarding something interesting
- Track: open rate, click rate, reply rate, forward rate

### Content Types That Work
1. **Behind-the-scenes** (what happened this week at Abugo)
2. **Curated insights** (best things we read + our take)
3. **Data stories** (one interesting number from our operations)
4. **Portfolio spotlights** (deep dive into one company's week)
5. **Industry analysis** (commerce SaaS trends from our vantage point)

## Blog

### Article Template
```
# [SEO Title - include primary keyword, under 60 chars]

**Meta description:** [Under 160 chars, include keyword, compelling]

*[Reading time] min read | [Date] | [Author]*

## [Introduction - H2]
[2-3 paragraphs hooking the reader and stating the thesis]

## [Section 1 - H2 with keyword variation]
[3-5 paragraphs with examples, data, and insights]

### [Subsection if needed - H3]
[Supporting detail]

## [Section 2 - H2]
[Next major point]

## [Section 3 - H2]
[Final major point or case study]

## Key Takeaways
- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]

## What's Next
[1-2 paragraphs with forward-looking perspective and CTA]

---

*[Author bio with link to LinkedIn]*
*[Related articles]*
```

### Best Practices
- **Publish Tuesday 8:00 CET** (best day for B2B blog indexing)
- 800-1500 words (sweet spot for SEO + readability)
- Include 1 internal link per 300 words
- Add alt text to all images
- Use schema markup for articles
- Update high-performing posts every 6 months

### SEO Keyword Clusters
1. **Commerce SaaS** -- "commerce SaaS Europe", "SaaS holding company", "commerce automation"
2. **Product categories** -- "booking software", "live chat e-commerce", "checkout optimization"
3. **Industry insights** -- "European SaaS market", "CEE tech ecosystem", "commerce trends 2026"
4. **AI in commerce** -- "AI chatbots e-commerce", "AI marketing automation", "headless commerce AI"

### Content Types That Work
1. **How-to guides** ("How to Build a Customer Feedback Loop Across 7 Products")
2. **Data-driven analysis** ("20M Bookings: What We Learned About No-Shows")
3. **Industry perspectives** ("The State of Commerce SaaS in Europe, 2026")
4. **Product philosophy** ("Why We Built Mira AI: The Case for AI-First Customer Support")
5. **Comparison/alternative posts** (SEO play for product discovery)
