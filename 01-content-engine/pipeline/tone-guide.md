# Abugo Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Voice Identity

Abugo speaks as a **builder-operator** -- someone who's in the trenches running 7 SaaS companies, not a consultant theorizing from the sidelines.

**Voice keywords:** Pragmatic, builder-minded, European, collaborative, transparent

## Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| LinkedIn thought leadership | Confident but humble | "We've learned this the hard way across 7 companies..." |
| Twitter/X threads | Sharp and direct | "Thread: 5 things nobody tells you about running a SaaS holding" |
| Newsletter | Conversational, insider | "This week in the Abugo universe..." |
| Blog | Authoritative, educational | "How commerce SaaS companies can leverage AI chatbots to..." |
| Customer stories | Warm, evidence-based | "When [Company] switched to Reservio, their no-show rate dropped 40%." |

## Writing Rules

### DO
- Lead with insight, not company name
- Use specific numbers ("20M bookings" not "millions of bookings")
- Show the work (process, decisions, trade-offs)
- Reference portfolio companies naturally, as examples of broader trends
- Write in first person plural ("we") for Abugo-level content
- Acknowledge challenges and failures -- it builds trust
- Use "commerce" not "e-commerce" when talking about the full ecosystem

### DON'T
- Don't use buzzwords without substance ("synergy", "disruption", "game-changer")
- Don't make every post a product pitch
- Don't ignore the European angle -- it's a differentiator
- Don't write generic SaaS content -- anchor everything in Abugo's specific experience
- Don't use corporate jargon ("leverage our solutions to optimize your...")
- Don't start posts with "Excited to announce..." or "Thrilled to share..."

## Platform-Specific Tone Adjustments

### LinkedIn
- Professional but not stiff
- Longer paragraphs OK (3-4 sentences)
- Use line breaks for readability
- End with a question or call to discussion
- Emoji: Minimal (1-2 max, strategic)

### Twitter/X
- Punchy, no filler words
- One idea per tweet in threads
- Use numbers and lists
- Conversational abbreviations OK
- Emoji: More acceptable, thread markers

### Newsletter
- Like writing to a smart friend
- Behind-the-scenes details welcome
- Can be longer and more nuanced
- Personal anecdotes from team encouraged
- Emoji: Section headers only

### Blog
- SEO-conscious but human-first
- Use headers, bullet points, pull quotes
- Include data and examples
- Link to portfolio company products naturally
- Emoji: None
